# Product Sense

## Product thesis

Retirement income planning is a confidence product, not a calculator product. The service must help households understand whether their future cash flow is resilient under plausible life events: retirement, pension start dates, rental vacancy, market drawdowns, medical cost increases, spouse death, and housing changes.

## Primary users

1. Pre-retiree households in their 40s to 60s.
2. Retired households evaluating sustainable monthly spending.
3. Advisors who need repeatable scenario evidence for client discussions.

## Core jobs to be done

- "Show me how much monthly income my household can expect by age."
- "Explain which inputs matter most to my retirement income."
- "Compare staying in my current home versus selling, downsizing, or renting it out."
- "Show whether my spouse is still protected if I die first."
- "Make the projection credible enough that I can act on it."

## Differentiators

- Household-level modeling rather than individual-only modeling.
- Asset-to-income conversion across real estate, pensions, savings, investments, and annuities.
- Scenario-first UX with clear confidence bands.
- Harness Engineering reliability practices embedded into model governance, testing, and release controls.

## Product principles

1. Explain assumptions before presenting conclusions.
2. Prefer ranges and stress cases over false precision.
3. Separate guaranteed income, expected income, and discretionary drawdown.
4. Make every recommendation auditable.
5. Treat privacy and financial safety as product features.
