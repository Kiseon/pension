# Product Sense

## Product thesis

Retirement income planning is a confidence product, not a calculator product.
The service focuses on Korean households and helps users understand whether
their future after-tax monthly income is resilient across work income,
retirement timing, pension start choices, IRP contributions, investment returns,
real estate net income, and housing-backed pension options.

## Primary users

1. Pre-retiree households in their 40s to 60s.
2. Retired households evaluating sustainable monthly spending.
3. Advisors who need repeatable scenario evidence for client discussions.

## Core jobs to be done

- "Show me how much monthly income my household can expect by age."
- "Explain which inputs matter most to my retirement income."
- "Show whether my target monthly income after retirement is reachable."
- "Recommend how to receive pensions and draw down assets to meet that target."
- "Show how much more I need to save before retirement if current assets are not enough."
- "Make the projection credible enough that I can act on it."

## Differentiators

- Household-level modeling rather than individual-only modeling.
- Korea-specific rule adapters for National Pension, retirement benefits, IRP,
  private pensions, housing-backed pension, tax, and financial asset drawdown.
- Asset-to-income conversion across employment, real estate net income,
  pensions, savings, investments, IRP, and annuities.
- Scenario-first UX with clear confidence bands.
- Harness Engineering reliability practices embedded into model governance, testing, and release controls.

## Product principles

1. Explain assumptions before presenting conclusions.
2. Prefer ranges and stress cases over false precision.
3. Separate guaranteed income, expected income, and discretionary drawdown.
4. Make every recommendation auditable.
5. Treat privacy and financial safety as product features.
